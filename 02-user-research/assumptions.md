This document outlines key assumptions made during the initial design phase. These assumptions help define user needs and guide early UX decisions in the absence of primary research.

---

## 1. Farmer Assumptions
- Farmers want **control over crop pricing** instead of accepting fixed mandi rates.
- Farmers prefer **secure and guaranteed payments** before or after delivery.
- Most farmers have **basic smartphone access** but limited technical expertise.
- Farmers value **simple workflows** with minimal steps and clear instructions.
- Trust in buyers increases when **contracts and payments are transparent**.

---

## 2. Buyer / Market Owner Assumptions
- Buyers want access to **verified farmers** to reduce risk and fraud.
- Buyers prefer **bulk purchasing** through predefined contracts.
- Buyers need **price clarity, quality assurance, and delivery tracking**.
- Buyers are willing to negotiate prices if **contracts are secure and enforceable**.

---

## 3. Consumer (Shop Users) Assumptions
- Consumers prefer **fresh, locally sourced agricultural products**.
- Simple browsing and checkout are more important than complex features.
- Trust increases when product origin and farmer details are visible.

---

## 4. Platform & System Assumptions
- Digital contracts increase trust for both farmers and buyers.
- Escrow-based payments reduce disputes and delayed payouts.
- Clear user flows reduce learning effort for low-tech users.
- A single platform serving B2B and B2C use cases is feasible if navigation is clear.

---

## 5. Design Constraints
- Interface must support **low literacy and low digital familiarity**.
- Navigation should be intuitive with minimal text dependency.
- Visual clarity is prioritized over heavy animations or complex interactions.

---

## Note
These assumptions will be validated and refined in later design stages through usability testing and stakeholder feedback.
